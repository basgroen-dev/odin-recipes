Description

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian dish made with spaghetti tossed in a rich, creamy sauce made from eggs, Parmesan cheese, pancetta or guanciale, and black pepper. The heat from the pasta cooks the eggs, creating a silky coating without the use of cream. It's a simple yet flavorful dish, known for its indulgent texture and savory taste.

Ingredients

  • Spathetti
  • Pancetta
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Garlic
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until crispy. If using garlic, add it to the skillet and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and grated Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  4. Quickly add the hot drained pasta to the skillet with the pancetta, tossing to combine. Remove from heat.
  5. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to reach desired consistency.
  6. Season with freshly ground black pepper and additional salt if needed.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese and black pepper. Enjoy!
